◆ wait()

bool email::WaitSet::wait ( const std::chrono::milliseconds  timeout = std::chrono::milliseconds(-1))

Wait.

Wait on the wait set.

A negative timeout value means to wait indefinitely. A timeout value equal to 0 means to do a non-blocking check and not wait. A positive timeout value will be used as a timeout.

This will wait until at least one object (subscription, client, or server) is ready, or until the timeout has been reached. The objects that are ready will have their respective pointers be non-nullptr in their respective vectors; the rest will have their pointers set to nullptr.

Waiting on an empty waitset with a timeout>=0 is similar to a sleep() call.
